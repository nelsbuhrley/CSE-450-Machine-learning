About this dashboard. The dataset is from a Portuguese bank's direct-marketing campaigns (UCI Bank Marketing). Each row is a phone contact made to a client. The goal is to predict whether a client subscribed a term deposit (y = yes/no). Use the filters and tabs to slice the data from many ML-relevant perspectives: distributions, conversion rates, numeric splits by class, correlations, and feature-signal (mutual information / lift).

Important: this dataset has no year column, and your file is a subset of UCI's data — so absolute years are inferred, not measured.

How it works: rows are assumed chronological. We walk forward and treat any month-index decrease (e.g. nov → may) as a year boundary. The number of detected boundaries tells us how many calendar years your subset spans. Below you can either (a) view time as relative — the subset's own start = month 1, end = month N, no absolute calendar year — or (b) anchor the last record to a specific calendar year/month if you have outside knowledge of when the campaign ended.

About pdays: only ~3% of records have a real value (the rest = -1 / 999 meaning "never previously contacted"). It tells you about this client's prior touch, not the absolute date.
